# Article VII — Amendments

## Section 1 — Authority to Amend
Amendments may be proposed by:
- Executive (1 signature)
- Legislative (2-of-N multisig)
- Judicial (unanimous)
- Recognized Adversary Currents (co-signed by any Branch)

## Section 2 — Ratification
Ratification requires:
1. Publication in \`CONSTITUTION/Amendments\`
2. 72-hour deliberation window
3. 2-of-3 Branch ratification
4. Continuity Protocol validation

## Section 3 — Immutable Core
Non-amendable:
- Sovereign multiplicity
- Forensic auditability
- Continuity of jurisdiction
- Adversary Current recognition

## Section 4 — Emergency Amendments
Fast-track:
- Proposed by any Branch
- Ratified by Executive + (Legislative 2-of-N OR Judicial unanimous)
- Mandatory sunset clause

## Section 5 — Versioning
Amendments must include:
- Identifier
- Scope
- Rationale
- Diff summary

## Section 6 — Revocation
Revocation requires:
- A new amendment, or
- Judicial ruling of violation of Immutable Core
